In this section, we learn that even some of the easier factoring strategies we have learned here and here can be extended to cover seemingly more difficult cases. For example, can you imagine that you actually already have a trick for factoring the following? \[ x^4 - 2x^2 +1 \] I'll work some examples for you that are similar. I'll show you three techniques, and you decide whichever one you prefer. You can use any of them.

Completely factor the following. \[ x^6 - 10x^3 + 24 \]

Answer, via Method 1:

Notice that this is like \[ w^2 - 10w +24 = (w-6)(w-4) \] After all, \[ x^6 - 10x^3 + 24 = (x^3)^2 - 10(x^3) + 24 \] See the similarity? The point is that, for any real number \(w\) \[ w^2 - 10w +24 = (w-6)(w-4) \] is true. Also, \(x^3\) is (the symbol for) a real number. So, this \(w\) equation is also true when \(x^3\) is substituted in for \(w\): \[ (x^3)^2 - 10x^3 + 24 = (x^3-6)(x^3-4) \] Get it? Since \((x^3)^2=x^3\cdot x^3 = x^6\), it's also true that \[ x^6 - 10x^3 + 24 = (x^3-6)(x^3-4) \]

Answer, via Method 2:

Use the "\(ac\)" method, which is explained in this section. That is, you first find a factorization of \(ac = 1\cdot 24 = 24\) into two numbers that add up to \(-10\). Those numbers are \(-4\) and \(-6\). Then, rewrite the middle term according to that breakdown and then factor by grouping: \begin{align*} x^6 - 10x^3 + 24 &= x^6 - 4x^3 - 6x^3 + 24 \\ &= x^3(x^3 - 4) - 6(x^3 - 4) \\ & = (x^3-6)(x^3 - 4) \end{align*}

Answer, via Method 3 (Guess and Check):

Guessing-and-checking might look like this: \[ x^6 - 10x^3 + 24 = (x^3 - \quad)(x^3 - \quad) \] You just sit there and think about how to fill in the blanks: You'd need two numbers that multiply together to give you \(24\) and add together to give you \(-10\)—which is the same thing you had to think about when factoring, say, \(w^2 - 10w + 24\). So, yup, \(-4\) and \(-6\) are the numbers: \[ x^6 - 10x^3 + 24 = (x^3 - 4)(x^3 - 6) \]

Important Last Step (applies to all 3):

Scrutinize the resulting factors, namely \(x^3-4\) and \(x^3 - 6\) and see if there any factoring methods that will apply to break these down further. This should really be understood relative to your knowledge base. The background knowledge of factoring assumed in this section is contained in the sections linked to in the first paragraph above. No methods described in those sections apply to these factors, so we're done.

Would you like to see one more without all the talk? I'll use the guess-and-check method, which is really my favorite.

Completely factor the following. \[ x^8 +7x^4 - 8 \]

Answer:

\begin{align*} x^8 +7x^4 - 8 & = (x^4 - \quad)(x^4 + \quad)\\ & = (x^4 - 1)(x^4 +8) \\ & = (x^2 - 1)(x^2+1)(x^4 + 8)\\ & = (x-1)(x+1)(x^2 +1)(x^4 + 8) \end{align*}
