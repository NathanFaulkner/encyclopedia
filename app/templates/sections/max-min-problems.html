We now turn to a skill concerning parabolas which has some nice real-world applications. The basic observation in this section is that, depending on whether a parabola opens up or opens down, there will always be a maximum \(y\)-value attained (if it opens down) or a minimum \(y\)-value (if it opens up). Let's practice this skill in a "pure" setting (and with easier numbers) before we jump into the applications.

Figure out whether the function has a maximum or a minimum, and figure out what that (\(y\)) value is and where (on the \(x\)-axis) it attains that value. \[ f(x)=-x^2+2x-12 \]

Answer:

To answer this, I am going to think a little bit about what I know the graph will look like, but mostly I will be applying my "Alternative Method to Find Vertex Form, II" from the section on quadratics in standard form. Since the coefficient on \(x^2\) is negative, we know the parabola opens down; so, it will have a maximum (\(y\)) value. This max value is, of course, attained at the vertex. I will use the "short-cut" method for finding the coordinates of the vertex. The vertex is at \[ x = \frac{-b}{2a} = \frac{-2}{2(-1)} = 1 \] and \[ y = f(1) = -1^2 +2\cdot 1 - 12 = -1 + 2 - 12 = -11 \] So, its max value is \(=-11\), attained at \(x=1\).

Figure out whether the function has a maximum or a minimum, and figure out what that (\(y\)) value is and where (on the \(x\)-axis) it attains that value. \[ f(x)=\frac{1}{2}x^2-7x+19 \]

Answer:

To answer this, I am going to think a little bit about what I know the graph will look like, but mostly I will be applying my "Alternative Method to Find Vertex Form, II" from the section on quadratics in standard form. Since the coefficient on \(x^2\) is positive, we know the parabola opens upwards; so, it will have a minimum (\(y\)) value. This min value is, of course, attained at the vertex. I will use the "short-cut" method for finding the coordinates of the vertex. The vertex is at \[ x = \frac{-b}{2a} = \frac{-(-7)}{2\left(\frac{1}{2}\right)} = \frac{7}{1} = 7 \] and \begin{align*} y = f(7) &= \frac{1}{2}(7)^2 - 7\cdot 7 + 19\\ &= \frac{1}{2}\cdot 49 - 49 + 19 \\ &= -\frac{1}{2}\cdot 49 + 19\\ &= -24.5 + 19\\ &= -5.5 \end{align*} So, its min value is \(=-5.5\), attained at \(x=7\).
