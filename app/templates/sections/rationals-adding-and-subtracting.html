In this section—as you can already see—we are adding and subtracting rational functions... which means, again, that you will first of all want to be sure you can add and subtract plain, old fractions.

Add or subtract the following fractions, and simplify (reduce to lowest terms).
  1. \( \frac{3}{4}+ \frac{8}{7}\)

    Answer:

    \[ \frac{3}{4}+ \frac{8}{7} = \frac{3\cdot 7}{4 \cdot 7} + \frac{8\cdot 4}{7\cdot 4} = \frac{21}{28} + \frac{32}{28} = \frac{53}{28} \]
  2. \(\frac{5}{3}+ \frac{7}{15}\)

    Answer:

    \[ \frac{5}{3}+ \frac{7}{15} = \frac{5\cdot 5}{3 \cdot 5} + \frac{7}{15} = \frac{25}{15} + \frac{7}{15} = \frac{32}{15} \]
  3. \(\frac{2}{9}+ \frac{5}{12}\)

    Answer:

    \[ \frac{2}{9}+ \frac{5}{12} = \frac{2\cdot 4}{9 \cdot 4} + \frac{5 \cdot 3}{12\cdot 3} = \frac{8}{36} + \frac{15}{36} = \frac{23}{36} \]
  4. \(\frac{5}{6}+ \frac{7}{15}\)

    Answer:

    \[ \frac{5}{6} + \frac{7}{15} = \frac{5\cdot 5}{6 \cdot 5} + \frac{7 \cdot 2}{15 \cdot 2} = \frac{25}{30} + \frac{14}{30} = \frac{39}{30} = \frac{13}{10} \]

Now, we just do "the same thing" but with rational functions: The key is to factor all the terms, to look for a common denominator, to multiply "top and bottom" as needed to create the common denominators, and then to add numerators. Let's practice.

Carry out the indicated operation (add or subtract). Reduce to lowest terms. \[ \frac{12}{x-1} + \frac{x - 4}{x-1} \]

Answer:

These two fractions already have a common denominator, so this one is really a little too easy. Just combine the numerators: \begin{align*} \frac{12}{x-1} + \frac{x - 4}{x-1} &= \frac{12 + x -4 }{x-1} &= \frac{x +8}{x-1} \end{align*}

Carry out the indicated operation (add or subtract). Reduce to lowest terms. \[ \frac{12}{x-1} - \frac{x-4}{x^2 - 1} \]

Answer:

This one may not look that different, but it is. Look! The denominator of the second fraction is \(x^2-1\). But if we factor it, we can see our way to making a common denominator. You have to multiply by a "form of 1"—that is, multiplying the same thing top and bottom—to change the way a ratio is written. I use that technique here to rewrite the first ratio in terms of the denominator of the second. Then, I can just subtract the numerators. \begin{align*} \frac{12}{x-1} - \frac{x-4}{x^2 - 1} &= \frac{12}{x-1} - \frac{x-4}{(x-1)(x+1)}\\ &= \frac{12}{(x-1)} \frac{(x+1)}{(x+1)} - \frac{x-4}{(x-1)(x+1)}\\ &= \frac{12x + 12}{(x-1)(x+1)} - \frac{x-4}{(x-1)(x+1)}\\ &= \frac{12x + 12 - (x-4)}{(x-1)(x+1)}\\ &= \frac{11x + 16}{(x-1)(x+1)} \end{align*}

Carry out the indicated operation (add or subtract). Reduce to lowest terms. \[ \frac{3x- 5}{x^2 +3x - 18} + \frac{4 - x}{x^2 - 36} \]

Answer:

I factor, then I create a common denominator, then I add the numerators, and then I factor the resulting numerator in case I get a factor in common with the denominator, which could then be canceled. \begin{align*} \frac{3x- 5}{x^2 +3x - 18} + \frac{4 - x}{x^2 - 36} &= \frac{3x- 5}{(x+6)(x-3)} + \frac{4 - x}{(x-6)(x+6)}\\ & = \frac{(3x- 5)}{(x+6)(x-3)} \cdot \frac{(x-6)}{(x-6)}+ \frac{(4 - x)}{(x-6)(x+6)} \cdot \frac{(x-3)}{(x-3)}\\ & = \frac{3x^2 - 23x + 30}{(x+6)(x-3)(x-6)} + \frac{-x^2+7x-12}{(x-6)(x+6)(x-3)}\\ & = \frac{2x^2 - 16x + 18}{(x-6)(x+6)(x-3)}\\ & = \frac{2(x^2 - 8x + 9)}{(x-6)(x+6)(x-3)}\\ \end{align*}
