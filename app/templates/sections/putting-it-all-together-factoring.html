Now, I bet you know the concept of factoring out a "greatest common factor" or GCF, but now we use that technique in concert with some of the other lessons we've learned, specifically,

  • factoring trinomials, part 1
  • special patterns,
  • factoring by grouping,
  • factoring trinomials, part 2,
  • and quadratic pattern.

Consider a few examples to get you headed in the right direction.

Completely factor the following. \[ x^6 - 8x^5 + 15x^4 \]

Answer:

Look for a GCF, and then use any or all of your factoring skills to finish it: \begin{align*} x^6 - 8x^5 + 15x^4 &= x^4(x^2 - 8x+15) \\ &= x^4(x-3)(x-5) \end{align*} After identifying the GCF, my next step was accomplished using the methods of the section, Factoring Trinomials, Part 1.

Completely factor the following. \[ 81 r^{2} x^{5} + 54 r t x^{5} + 9 t^{2} x^{5} \]

Answer:

Look for a GCF, and then use any or all of your factoring skills to finish it: \begin{align*} 81 r^{2} x^{5} + 54 r t x^{5} + 9 t^{2} x^{5} &= 9x^5(9r^2-6rt+t^2)\\ & = 9x^5((3r)^2 - 2 \cdot 3r \cdot t + t^2) \\ & = 3x^2(3r + t)^2\\ \end{align*} The hard part here is to realize that \[ 9r^2-6rt+t^2 = (3r)^2 - 2 \cdot 3r \cdot t + t^2 \] follows the special patterns, of \[ A^2 + 2 AB + B^2 \] and thus factors into the form \((A+B)^2\). My \(A\) in this case is \(3r\) and my \(B\) is \(t\).

Completely factor the following. \[ 28 x^{6} - 112 x^{5} + 21 x^{4} - 84 x^{3} \]

Answer:

Look for a GCF, and then use any or all of your factoring skills to finish it: \begin{align*} 28 x^{6} - 112 x^{5} + 21 x^{4} - 84 x^{3} &= 7x^3(4x^3-16x^2+3x - 12)\\ & = 7x^3\left(4x^2(x - 4) + 3(x-4)\right) \\ & = 7x^3\left(4x^2 + 3\right)\left(x-4)\right) \\ \end{align*} How did I figure out that the GCF included \(7\)?&mdash---answer: basically just by inspection and reinspection of each coefficient until something worked out. That is, I slowly realized I could factor out \(7\) and then when I had done that I found that there were no common factors left. The next step was done by factoring by grouping,

Completely factor the following. \[ -8x^4+2x^3+6x^2 \]

Answer:

Look for a GCF, and then use any or all of your factoring skills to finish it: \begin{align*} -8x^4+2x^3+6x^2 &= -2x^2(4x^2 - x - 3) \\ & = -2x^2(4x^2 - 4x + 3x - 3)\\ &= -2x^2\big(4x(x-1)+3(x-1)\big)\\ &= -2x^2(4x+3)(x-1) \end{align*} After identifying the GCF, my next step was accomplished using the methods of the section, Factoring Trinomials, Part 2,

Completely factor the following. \[ 3x^{10} - 9x^6 +6x^2 \]

Answer:

Look for a GCF, and then use any or all of your factoring skills to finish it: \begin{align*} 3x^{10} - 9x^6 +6x^2 &= 3x^2(x^8-3x^4+2)\\ & = 3x^2(x^4 - \quad)(x^4 - \quad)\\ & = 3x^2(x^4 - 2)(x^4 - 1)\\ & = 3x^2(x^4 - 2)(x^2 - 1)(x^2+1)\\ & = 3x^2(x^4 - 2)(x- 1)(x+1)(x^2+1)\\ \end{align*} After identifying the GCF, my next step was accomplished using the methods of the section on recognizing a "quadratic pattern".
