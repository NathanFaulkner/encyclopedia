There is nothing earth-shattering here! All we have to do is "combine like terms." So, I expect you will get through this part quickly. Yet, I will advocate a special approach to this very easy task—of lining up like terms in columns—because this will help you in the harder tasks of multiplying and, especially, dividing polynomials.

Carry out the indicated operation (i.e., addition): \[ \left(-3x^4 + 4x^3 - 2x^2 + 1\right) + \left(2x^4 +6x^2 - x- 5\right) \]

Answer:

Just combine like terms! But, take my advice, and start rewriting the polynomials above each other with like terms lined up in columns, like so: \[ \begin{matrix} &-3x^4 & + & 4x^3 & - & 2x^2 & & & +& 1\\ +&2x^4 & & & + & 6x^2 & -& x& - & 5 \\ \hline &-x^4 & + & 4x^3 & + & 4x^2 & -& x& - & 4 \\ \end{matrix} \]

Carry out the indicated operation (i.e., subtraction): \[ \left(-3x^4 + 4x^3 - 2x^2 + 1\right) - \left(2x^4 +6x^2 - x- 5\right) \]

Answer:

Be sure to "distribute" the minus to the terms in the second polynomial as you subtract. Here is the answer using my "column" method (which, again, will be especially useful if not almost required when we get to division of polynomials.) \[ \begin{matrix} &-3x^4 & + & 4x^3 & - & 2x^2 & & & +& 1 \\ -&(2x^4 & & & + & 6x^2 & - & x& - & 5 )\\ \hline &-5x^4 & + & 4x^3 & - & 8x^2 & + & x& + & 6 \\ \end{matrix} \]
