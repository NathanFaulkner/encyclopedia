We now to turn to some applications of linear systems. I hope you will find at least some of these examples very striking—we are solving some pretty legit problems here! I have about 4 different types of problems in mind (but there are oh-so-many more applications of linear systems). I have provided examples for 3 of them here. Good news! we can use our TI's to solve these! Your job in each problem will be to set up the system that you want to solve, and then turn the solution over to the TI—which can perform elimination for us!! I will show you how.

Say that we have a quantity of 96% gold alloy (the rest is silver), and we want to mix it with a 50% gold alloy to create a portion of 70% gold alloy. If we want 4 ounces of the 70% gold alloy, how much of each of the original alloys should we combine? You may round your answer to two decimal places, as long as you don't round until the very last step. (Use exact fractions until the last step.)

Answer:

Let \(x\) represent the number of ounces of the 96% gold alloy that we will need, and let \(y\) represent the number of ounces of the 50% gold alloy that we will need. Since we want to combine the old alloys to form 4 ounces of the 70%% gold alloy, we know that \[ x + y = 4 \] Also, we know that the quantity of gold in the final mixture will be the gold from the 96% alloy plus the gold from the 50% alloy, we have that \[ 0.96 x + 0.5y = 0.7\cdot 4 = 2.8 \] So, we need to find the solution to the system \[ \begin{cases} x + y = 4\\ 0.96 x + 0.5 y = 2.8 \end{cases} \] Here is the cool part: On your TI graphign calculator, find the button marked \(x^{-1}\). The "2nd" option on that button is called MATRX for `"matrix.'' Hit that button to open the MATRX menu. Next, use the arrow keys to toggle to the \texttt{EDIT} tab. Select matrix [A]. Enter the dimensions as 2 x 3. Then enter the coefficients of your matrix like so:
[ 1 1 4   ]
[ 0.960.5 2.8 ]
Next, you go back to the MATRX menu (hit "[2nd] [\(x^{-1}\)]" again) and toggle to the MATH tab. Here, you will scroll down until you find rref(. (FYI: "rref" stands for "reduced row echeleon form," not that it matters.) Now, hit [2nd] [\(x^{-1}\)] (MATRX) again, and, under NAMES, select [A]. Hit [ENTER] twice. You will see something like the following displayed:
[ 1 0 1.7391304348 ]
[ 0 1 2.2608695652 ]
The top number in the far right column reveals the value of \(x\), and the bottom number in the far right column reveals the value of \(y\). That is, rounded, the final answer is that we would need about 2.26 ounces of the 50% alloy and about 1.74 ounces of the 96% alloy.

I won't give quite as much explanation in the next example:

"We lost the receipts": For the opening day of the fair, (let's say that) 10,892 admission tickets were sold. The receipts totaled $99,516. Tickets for children cost $7 each and adults' cost $15 each. How many of each type of ticket were sold?

Answer:

Let \(x\) represent he number of child's tickets that were sold and \(y\) represent the number of adult's tickets that were sold. Since the total number of tickets sold was 10,892, you have that \[ x + y = 10892 \] Next, using the prices of each and the total revenue of $99,516, you get that \[ 7 x + 15 y = 99516 \] Then your system is \[ \begin{cases} x + y = 10892\\ 7 x + 15 y = 99516 \end{cases} \] Put into your TI's MATRX editor the following:
[ 1 1 10892 ]
[ 715 99516 ]
Do rref(, and you get:
[ 1 0 7983 ]
[ 01 2909 ]
Interpret this to get your final answer: 7983 child tickets were sold, and 2909 adult tickets were sold.

Next, we look at an example that results in an answer of "no solution."

A typical direct flight from Raleigh to San Francisco takes 5 hours and 48.5 minutes. However, the return flight from San Francisco to Raleigh takes 4 hours and 58 minutes. The difference between the two can be accounted for by the wind blowing against the plane as it flies west (the prevailing winds blow east) but actually adding to the plane's speed as it travels back east! We are assuming an "average" wind speed, which means we regard the wind speeds coming and going as the same (Indeed, I noted on a flight from Seattle to Atlanta in 2019 that the plane was flying about 40 mph over its top speed! That was because the wind was helping it.) The distance between these two cities is about 2410 miles. We are going to find the average wind speed and the average "apparent" speed of the plane (relative to the air around it—which you can think of as a measure of how hard the plane's engines are having to work to push through the air) across a two-way trip. The key here is to realize that you can add the wind speed to the plane's speed for the return (west to east trip) to get the plane's "actual" speed (relative to the ground), but you must subtract the wind speed from the plane's apparent speed on the trip from east to west (as the wind is working against the plane's direction of travel). You can round your final answer to two decimal places. However, you MUST include four decimal places for intermediate steps.

Answer:

You will need to use the basic fact that the rate of travel in miles per hour times the number of hours traveled gives you the distance traveled in miles. Note that the time in hours for the east to west trip took (in hours) \[ 5 + \frac{48.5}{60} \approx 5.8083 \] and the trip from west to east took (in hours) \[ 4 + \frac{58}{60} \approx 4.9667 \] Let \(v\) represent the (average) apparent speed of the plane (relative to the air around it), and let \(w\) represent the (average) wind speed. So, we get two equations—one for the trip from east to west: \[ 5.8083\left(v-w\right) = 2410 \] —and one for the trip from west to east: \[ 4.9667\left(v+w\right) = 2410 \] You can distribute these values out to obtain the system \[ \begin{cases} 5.8083 v - 5.8083 w = 2410 \\ 4.9667 v + 4.9667 w = 2410 \end{cases} \] You could do elimination—or just let the calculator handle it! I entered:
[ 5.8083 -5.8083 2410 ]
[ 4.9667 4.9667 2410 ]
Performing rref( on the TI, I got
[ 1 0 450.08 ]
[ 01 35.15 ]
Therefore, the average speed of the plane was 450.08 mph, and the average wind speed was 35.15 mph.
