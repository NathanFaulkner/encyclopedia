This chapter is about quadratic functions. But what is a quadratic function? Basically, it is a function which has an "\(x^2\)" in its formula but no higher powers. So, for example, \[ f(x) = 3x^2 - 2x +5 \] is a quadratic function, but \[ g(x) = 3x^3 - x^2 +x - 1 \] is not, because "\(x^3\)" is a higher power, isn't it?

Topics covered in this chapter include the study of quadratic functions from three perspectives. These perspectives are the key to understanding basically all the math in the high school curriculum (through calculus), too!

  • The Algebraic Perspective: This is working with functions symbolically, using variables, equations, and other symbols.
  • The Graphical Perspective: We'll graph quadratic functions just like, elsewhere, we hav graphed linear functions and absolute value functions.
  • The Inuitive or Real-World Perspectives: What sorts of applications are quadratic functions relevant to? Also, how does the concept "live" in your mind? How do you process what it's all about?
